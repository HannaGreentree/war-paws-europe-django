From Sirens to Safety

The war in Ukraine is the largest war in Europe and animal crisis since World War II. Thousands of pets have lost their owners. Animals are living in constant danger — exposed to cold, hunger, stress, and daily explosions. It is not too late to save an abandoned cat or dog from the war zones of Ukraine. Thousands of pets have lost their owners. Many were left behind during evacuation, while others were already living on the streets. These animals survive under bombardment, without regular access to food or water. Temperatures can fall to –20°C, and weakened animals face constant threats, including attacks from other animals. By choosing to adopt a pet from a war zone, you are not only giving an animal a home — you are giving them safety, warmth, and a chance to live.

Created by volunteers and animal lovers

War Paws Europe was created by volunteers who want to make adoption and pet travel clearer, safer, and more achievable.

Many animals are left behind during evacuations. Some survive near ruined buildings and explosions. This site collects practical steps, not just stories.

Who this website is for

People abroad who want to adopt from Ukraine, and Ukrainians who need guidance to travel with their own pets. We gather documents, contacts, and examples in one place.

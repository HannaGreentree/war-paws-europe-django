Documents, Vets & Delivery Costs

This page explains the typical steps and documents needed to adopt a cat or dog from Ukraine and travel abroad. Requirements may differ by country, so always confirm the latest rules for your destination.

Step-by-Step Adoption Process (2025)

  1. 1) Select a shelter or volunteer

    Choose a shelter or volunteer you trust. Check their social media, activity history, comments, receipts, and reports. Contact them directly and ask questions.

  2. 2) Confirm travel route and destination rules

    Rules depend on your destination (EU, UK, USA, etc.). Ask the volunteer what documents can be prepared locally and what must be handled after the animal arrives.

  3. 3) Mandatory medical preparation

    • Microchip: must be done first.
    • Rabies vaccination: recorded in documents.
    • Rabies titer test: blood sample taken at least 30 days after vaccination and sent to an approved lab.
    • Waiting period: some countries require additional waiting time after the blood test.

  4. 4) Documents and certificates

    Most rescued animals do not have documents. Volunteers can help, but it is usually the adopter’s responsibility to pay for medical tests and official paperwork.

    • EU / International Pet Passport: issued after chip + vaccine records.
    • Health certificate / export documents: may be required shortly before travel.
    • Country-specific forms: check official rules for your destination.

  5. 5) Arrange transport

    In most cases, you do not need to travel to Ukraine. Volunteers may help organise travel, and you pay the transport company directly. Some animals may travel better if the adopter collects them in a safer region.

  6. 6) Travel crate / carrier

    A suitable crate or carrier is usually paid by the adopter. Sometimes a volunteer may provide one for free, but you should not expect this.

How can I pay for the vet clinic?

  • Option 1: You pay the clinic directly (the clinic provides bank details), and the volunteer takes the animal to the appointment.
  • Option 2: The volunteer pays first, then sends you receipts, and you transfer the money back. However, many volunteers cannot take this risk, so do not expect it.

Language barrier

Do not be afraid if someone speaks Ukrainian and not English. Some Ukrainians speak basic English, and for others you can use Google Translate or AI translation. It is simple and should not stop you from saving a cat or dog.

Borders and common routes

Ukraine borders:

  • Poland
  • Slovakia
  • Hungary
  • Moldova

Poland is often used due to strong volunteer networks. Some countries may allow entry with microchipping and vaccination completed after crossing, but always confirm current rules.

Typical costs (examples)

Costs depend on the city in Ukraine and your destination country. You pay 0 money for the animal itself. Main costs are vet preparation, transport, and a travel crate/carrier.

  • Microchip: 500–700 UAH
  • Rabies vaccination: 150–300 UAH
  • Rabies titer test: 2,500 UAH (standard) / 5,000 UAH (urgent)
  • EU / international passport: 50–400 UAH
  • Courier to lab (if needed): 100–250 UAH
  • Transport to border / inside Ukraine: variable
  • Transport to your country: variable (example: £300–£1,500 EU → UK road transport)
  • Travel crate / carrier: paid by adopter (unless volunteer provides one)

Important safety note

Unfortunately, not every person who calls themselves a volunteer is trustworthy. Always check social media activity, past posts, comments, and transparency. If someone requests money in a suspicious way, step back and verify carefully.
